Product Discovery

From assumption to insight.

I logged every job search session for two weeks. The data told a different story than my intuition.

The Wrong Problem

I thought I needed to apply to more jobs.

I started with a simple hypothesis: job searching is a numbers game. If I applied to more roles, I'd get more interviews. I was optimizing for volume, not outcomes.

The Real Problem

It was a systems problem.

I spent 70% of my time on coordination—not strategic work. The insight: The best candidates had better systems, not more discipline.

Key Insight
The opportunity was eliminating coordination work, not optimizing it.
